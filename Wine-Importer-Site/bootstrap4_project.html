Our Partners

Château Smith Haut Lafitte

Château Smith Haut Lafitte boasts wine that is as beautiful as the 14th-century chateau it is named after. The vineyard area consists of 67 hectares (170 acres), 56 hectares of which are planted with grape varieties of 55% Cabernet Sauvignon, 35% Merlot and 10% Cabernet Franc. The remaining 11 hectares are cultivated with white varieties of 90% Sauvignon blanc, 5% Sémillon and 5% Sauvignon gris. Ranked among the "Grand Cru" class within the Pessac-Leognan appellation, it is impossible to go wrong with wine from Château Smith Haut Lafitte.

Château Margaux

In 1973, the French Government deemed Château Margaux to be a national treasure, its wine is no different. The domaine of Château Margaux extends 262 hectares (650 acres), of which 87 hectares (210 acres) are entitled to the Margaux AOC declaration. 80 hectares (200 acres) are planted with 75% Cabernet Sauvignon, 20% Merlot, with 2% Cabernet Franc and Petit verdot. 12 hectares (30 acres) are cultivated with Sauvignon blanc to make the dry white Pavillon Blanc. Boasting a wine pedigree that dates back to 1500, Chateau Margaux's wine has withstood the test of time.

Château Gassier

Château Gassier is situated in the heart of the Côtes de Provence Sainte-Victoire appellation. It covers 40 hectares of vines cultivated using organic practices and bordered by the Sainte-Victoire mountain in the North, the Sainte Baume mountains in the South, and the Monts Auréliens in the East. The vineyards are within 30 Km. of the Mediterranean Sea and have a continental climate perfect for cultivating vines. Vermentino, Syrah, and Grenache are the main grape varieties grown here used to make white and their famous rose wines. If you're looking for rose, look no further than the Gassier pink label!
